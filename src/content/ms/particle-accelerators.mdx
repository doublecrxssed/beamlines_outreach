# Particle Accelerators

How do scientists know what fundamental particles exist if they are too small to see? The answer is simple, yet brutal: we smash them together at the speed of light.

To understand what a car is made of, you could carefully take it apart with a wrench. Or, you could crash two cars together at 600 miles per hour and look at the pieces that fly off. Physicists do the latter.

To achieve these incredible speeds, scientists build machines called **Particle Accelerators**. The most famous is the Large Hadron Collider, a 27-kilometer ring buried under Europe. But how do you push a particle? You can't just hit it with a bat.

Wait... particles are incredibly tiny, but many of them have an Electrical Charge (like a proton). This means we can push and pull them using invisible forces!

### The Cyclotron

A **Cyclotron** is an early type of particle accelerator that uses two very specific forces to trap a particle and whip it up to high speeds. 

We need two tools to drive our particle:
1. **The Electric Field:** This acts like the gas pedal. It pushes the particle, making it move faster and gain energy.
2. **The Magnetic Field:** This acts like the steering wheel. It doesn't speed the particle up; it just bends its path into a circle, keeping it trapped inside the machine.

**Your Goal:** You are the operator of this Cyclotron. We need to successfully eject the proton into the target chamber.
*   **Step 1:** Push the **Magnetic Field** slider up halfway. Notice how the particle gets trapped in a circular orbit.
*   **Step 2:** Now, slowly push the **Electric Field** (gas pedal) slider up. Watch the particle gain energy (glow orange) and spiral outward!
*   **Step 3:** If the Magnetic Steering is too weak while the Electric Speed is too high, the particle will crash into the wall! Tune both sliders above 85% to successfully eject the particle.
